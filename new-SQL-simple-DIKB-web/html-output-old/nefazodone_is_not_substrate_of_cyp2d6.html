Assertion: nefazodone is_not_substrate_of cyp2d6

Evidence
Evidence For (item 0) Evidence Type: EV_EX_Met_Enz_ID_Cyp450_Hum_Recom Pointer: http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11872324 Reviewer: boycer
Quote: Enzyme system: recombinant human enzymes in human lymphoblastoid for CYP1A2 and CYP2D6; recombinant human enzymes in baculovirus-infected insect cells for CYP2C8, CYP2C9, and CYP2C19
NADPH added:yes

inhibitor used: no

When NEF or OH-NEF was incubated with microsomes from cells transfected with human cDNA for one of CYP1A1, CYP1A2, CYP2A6, CYP2D6, CYP3A4, CYP2C8, CYP2C9 arg, CYP2C9 cys, or CYP2C19, metabolite formation was seen with the CYP3A4 incubations only (Table 4).
Assumptions:
Evidence For (item 1) Evidence Type: EV_EX_Met_Enz_ID_Cyp450_Hum_Microsome_Chem Pointer: http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11872324 Reviewer: boycer
Quote:
Enzyme system: human liver microsomes:

NADPH added: yes

inhibitor used: quinidine

reaction: nefazodone --> OH-NEF ; nefazodone --> mCPP

Quote:
Incubations of human liver microsomes with NEF and the CYP3A4 inhibitor ketoconazole resulted in a concentration-dependent inhibition of the formation of mCPP and OH-NEF (Fig. 3). Incubations of human liver microsomes with NEF and the CYP2D6 inhibitor quinidine did not affect the production of OH-NEF and mCPP. Incubations of OH-NEF with the CYP3A4 inhibitor ketoconazole resulted in the inhibition of metabolite formation, and incubations with the CYP2D6 inhibitor quinidine did not, similar to what was found with NEF metabolism (data not presented).
Assumptions:
No evidence against!
