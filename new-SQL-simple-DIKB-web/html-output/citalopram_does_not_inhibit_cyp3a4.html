Assertion: citalopram does_not_inhibit cyp3a4

Evidence
Evidence For (item 0) Evidence Type: Non_traceable_Drug_Label_Statement Pointer: http://dbmi-icode-01.dbmi.pitt.edu/linkedSPLs/resource/structuredProductLabelMetadata/62f9e5da-6215- Reviewer: boycer
Quote: In vitro enzyme inhibition data did not reveal an inhibitory effect of citalopram on CYP3A4, -2C9, or -2E1, but did suggest that it is a weak inhibitor of CYP1A2, -2D6, and -2C19. Citalopram would be expected to have little inhibitory effect on in vivo metabolism mediated by these cytochromes. However, in vivo data to address this question are limited.
Assumptions:
Evidence For (item 1) Evidence Type: EV_EX_Met_Enz_Inhibit_Cyp450_Hum_Microsome Pointer: http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454728 Reviewer: boycer
Quote:

Enzyme system: human liver microsomes

NADPH added: yes (a dehydrogenase generating system)

probe reaction: triazolam hydroxylation

Quote:

CYP3A. CT and metabolites all were very weak or negligible inhibitors of CYP3A, as indicated by triazolam hydroxylation. None of the compounds (at 100micM) produced more than 50% inhibition. Fluvoxamine and nefazodone were moderately strong inhibitors (von
Moltke et al., 1996b, 1999c).
Assumptions:
Evidence For (item 2) Evidence Type: EV_PK_DDI_NR Pointer: http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10907965 Reviewer: boycer
Quote:

route of administration: oral

study duration: Subjects received triazolam 0.25 mg alone and another 0.25-mg dose after 4 weeks of citalopram 20 mg/day for 1 week, followed by 3 weeks of citalopram 40 mg/day

population: 17 (7 men and 10 women) completed the study)

tested for known CYP450 polymorphisms?

ages: 18 - 31

description:
The pharmacokinetics of triazolam and its metabolite alpha-hydroxytriazolam were unchanged by citalopram coadministration.

No evidence against!
