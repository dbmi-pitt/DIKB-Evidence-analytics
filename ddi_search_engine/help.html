Frequently Asked Questions
How do I find out about an interaction? Enter the prescribing drug and possible interacting drug. The pharmacogenetics trial bank queries online databases for relevant information regarding this particular interaction. You can select to limit or expand your query by trial type. Select the types of trials you wish to have returned by checking the appropriate check boxes. The default return is all types of trials.
What is the difference between the types of trials?

Randomized Controlled Trials -- These trials have random assignment and a control arm.

Epidemiological studies -- These trials are mostly observational and are do not have random assignment

Case Studies -- These trials are usually accounts of a few patients observed by a clinician.

Pharmokinetics in vitro -- These trials measure concentration of a drugs active ingredient or possible toxicity in a lab setting. The experiments are not conducted on live human/

Pharmokinetics in vivo -- These trials measure concentration of a drug's active ingredient or possible toxicity in a human.
